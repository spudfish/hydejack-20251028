  <h1>Biography</h1>
  <p markdown="1">
  **Tal McThenia** is the co-author of [*A Case for Solomon: Bobby Dunbar and the Kidnapping That Haunted a Nation*](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/A-Case-for-Solomon/Tal-McThenia/9781439173109) (with **Margaret Dunbar Cutright**; Simon & Schuster), an award-winning work of historical nonfiction about a notorious case of kidnapping and contested identity that divided the American South in the early twentieth century. The book was based on [*The Ghost of Bobby Dunbar*](https://www.thisamericanlife.org/352/the-ghost-of-bobby-dunbar), Tal’s one-hour documentary for the public radio series *This American Life*.
  </p>
  <p markdown="1">
  Most recently, Tal collaborated with legendary actor **Dick Van Dyke** to write [*100 Rules for Living to 100: An Optimist’s Guide to a Happy Life*](https://www.amazon.com/100-Rules-Living-Optimists-Guide/dp/1538777908) (Grand Central Publishing) and with acclaimed interior designer **Young Huh** on her debut book [*A Mood, A Thought, A Feeling*](https://www.rizzoliusa.com/book/9780847876327/) (Rizzoli). He has ghostwritten several other books across multiple genres.
  </p>
  <p markdown="1">
  His writing has appeared in *VanityFair.com*, *Audubon Magazine*, *American Express Departures*, *Bloomberg Businessweek*, *Popula*, and *Atlas Obscura*, among other venues. His work as a screenwriter includes *Shift*, a PBS drama; science mysteries for *Mosa Mack Science*; and the Nick Jr. series *Butterbean’s Café*.
  </p>
  <p markdown="1">
  Tal grew up in Rockbridge County, Virginia. He lives in New York City and upstate New York with his boyfriend, the writer [**Brett Berk**](https://brettberk.com).
  </p>
