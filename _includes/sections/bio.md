<h2>Biography</h2>

<p>Tal McThenia is the co-author of 'A Case for Solomon: Bobby Dunbar and the Kidnapping that Haunted A Nation’ (with Margaret Dunbar Cutright; Simon & Schuster), an award-winning historical non-fiction account of a notorious case of kidnapping and contested identity that divided the American South in the early 20th century. The book was based on ‘The Ghost of Bobby Dunbar,” Tal’s one-hour documentary for the public radio series This American Life.</p>

<p markdown="1">Most recently, Tal collaborated with legendary actor Dick Van Dyke to write [100 Rules for Living to 100: An Optimist’s Guide to a Happy Life](https://www.amazon.com/100-Rules-Living-Optimists-Guide/dp/1538777908) (Grand Central Publishing) and with acclaimed interior designer Young Huh on her debut book [A Mood, A Thought, A Feeling](https://www.rizzoliusa.com/book/9780847876327/) (Rizzoli). He has ghost-written several other books across multiple genres.
</p>
<p markdown="1">His writing has appeared in VanityFair.com, Audubon Magazine, American Express Departures, Bloomberg Businessweek, Popula, and Atlas Obscura, among other venues. His work as a screenwriter includes: 'Shift', a PBS drama; science mysteries for Mosa Mack Science; and the Nick, Jr. series Butterbean’s Café.</p>

<p markdown="1">Tal grew up in Rockbridge County, Virginia. He lives in New York City and upstate New York with his boyfriend, the writer [Brett Berk](https://brettberk.com).</p>
